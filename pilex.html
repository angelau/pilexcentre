THE RIV3 PROJECT

For over 70 years, SHELL, TOTAL, AGIP and other multi-national oil companies have exploited the crude oil and gas resources of the peoples of Niger Delta in a rather crude manner that has aggressively polluted and spoiled the environment; killing many species of plants and animals and chasing the survivors away. Community dwellers have lost much of their fishing and farming livelihood and the hydrocarbon poisoning in our foods, soil, water, air and body has led to sickness, diseases and death of our People. PILEX CENTRE with support from Advocates for Community Alternatives and Public Interest Lawyering Initiatives for West Africa in her RIV 3 Project, have engaged PUBLIC HEALTH and ENVIRONMENTAL EXPERTS to investigate the extent of the negative impact on the people and their environment. The outcome of this report will serve as a veritable tool for advocacy and litigations where necessary in view of securing Justice for impacted communities and setting a new standard for future engagement.

CLIENT INTERVIEW

Mrs. Chioma Bright (A widow) and her daughter visited Pilex Centre to seek Justice. Mrs. Chioma lost her husband sometime in 2022 and her step children threw her out of her matrimonial home and locked it. They petitioned her to the Police on trumped charges of Murder, theft of her husband’s property etc. Two of our lawyers accompanied them to interview the deputy Commissioner of Police and same day she was restored to her home after a year and some months of being refugee and severe psychological trauma which led to her admission to a government psychiatric facility in Port Harcourt. Pilex is still working on this matter and taking steps to see that her properties and that of her son are restored’ Furthermore, we are working to ensure that her heritage in her late husband’s company is duly remitted to her and her children, including approaching the Court to help her get justice.

Mark Chernaik of E-Law
